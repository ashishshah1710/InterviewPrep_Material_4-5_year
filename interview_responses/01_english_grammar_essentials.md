## **1. Tenses**  
Understanding verb tenses is crucial for describing actions in time. Letâs examine present, past, future, and perfect tenses.

### **Present Tenses**

- **Simple Present**: Used for facts, habits, and routines.
    - **Correct:** She writes code every day.
    - **Incorrect:** She writing code every day.

- **Present Continuous**: For actions happening now.
    - **Correct:** I am fixing the bug.
    - **Incorrect:** I is fixing the bug.

- **Present Perfect**: For actions that happened at an unspecified time, or actions with results in the present.
    - **Correct:** We have deployed the update.
    - **Incorrect:** We has deployed the update.

- **Present Perfect Continuous**: For actions started in the past and continue in the present.
    - **Correct:** He has been testing the new module since morning.
    - **Incorrect:** He have been testing the new module since morning.

#### **Examples**
1. Correct: The application runs smoothly.  
   Incorrect: The application run smoothly.

2. Correct: I am learning Python.  
   Incorrect: I am learn Python.

---

### **Past Tenses**

- **Simple Past**: For completed actions in the past.
    - **Correct:** I logged into the server yesterday.
    - **Incorrect:** I log into the server yesterday.

- **Past Continuous**: For ongoing actions in the past.
    - **Correct:** We were discussing the requirements.
    - **Incorrect:** We discussing the requirements.

- **Past Perfect**: For actions completed before another past action.
    - **Correct:** She had fixed the error before the meeting.
    - **Incorrect:** She fixed the error before the meeting.

- **Past Perfect Continuous**: For duration of actions up to a point in the past.
    - **Correct:** They had been optimizing the code for two hours before lunch.
    - **Incorrect:** They have been optimizing the code for two hours before lunch.

#### **Examples**
3. Correct: The server crashed at midnight.  
   Incorrect: The server crashes at midnight.

4. Correct: I was reviewing the documentation.  
   Incorrect: I was reviewed the documentation.

---

### **Future Tenses**

- **Simple Future**: For actions that will happen.
    - **Correct:** I will deploy the patch tomorrow.
    - **Incorrect:** I deploy the patch tomorrow.

- **Future Continuous**: For ongoing actions in the future.
    - **Correct:** She will be presenting at 10 AM.
    - **Incorrect:** She presenting at 10 AM.

- **Future Perfect**: Action completed before a specific future time.
    - **Correct:** By noon, we will have delivered the release.
    - **Incorrect:** By noon, we will delivered the release.

#### **Examples**
5. Correct: We will discuss the issue in the meeting.  
   Incorrect: We discuss the issue in the meeting.

6. Correct: I will be working late tonight.  
   Incorrect: I will working late tonight.

## **2. Subject-Verb Agreement**

Ensure that the subject and verb agree in number (singular/plural).

- **Correct:** The team is working hard.
- **Incorrect:** The team are working hard.

Common Rules:
- Singular subjects take singular verbs.
- Plural subjects take plural verbs.
- Collective nouns (team, group, etc.) are usually singular in American English.

#### **Examples**
7. Correct: The data is accurate.  
   Incorrect: The data are accurate.

8. Correct: The developers are efficient.  
   Incorrect: The developers is efficient.

9. Correct: There is a bug in the code.  
   Incorrect: There are a bug in the code.

10. Correct: There are bugs in the code.  
    Incorrect: There is bugs in the code.

## **3. Articles (a, an, the)**

Articles help clarify meaning.

- **A**: before words starting with a consonant sound.
    - Correct: I need a laptop.
    - Incorrect: I need laptop.

- **An**: before words starting with a vowel sound.
    - Correct: She is an analyst.
    - Incorrect: She is analyst.

- **The**: for specific things.
    - Correct: The report is ready.
    - Incorrect: Report is ready.

**Common Indian English Mistakes:**
- Omitting articles.
- Using articles incorrectly with uncountable nouns.

#### **Examples**
11. Correct: He is an engineer.  
    Incorrect: He is engineer.

12. Correct: The server is down.  
    Incorrect: Server is down.

13. Correct: Can you check the file?  
    Incorrect: Can you check file?

## **4. Prepositions**

Prepositions are words conveying location, time, and movement (in, on, at, for, since, to, by, with).

- **Correct:** I am at the office.
- **Incorrect:** I am in office.

Common Prepositions:
- **at** (specific location): at the gate, at the table
- **in** (inside/period): in the room, in the morning
- **on** (surface/days): on the desk, on Monday

**Common Mistakes:**
- Confusing "in" and "at"
- Wrong use of "since" vs "for"

#### **Examples**
14. Correct: I will call you on Monday.  
    Incorrect: I will call you in Monday.

15. Correct: She has worked here for two years.  
    Incorrect: She has worked here since two years.

16. Correct: Please send the email to me.  
    Incorrect: Please send the email me.

17. Correct: The report is on the table.  
    Incorrect: The report is at the table.

## **5. Indian English: Common Grammar Mistakes**

**1. Incorrect use of Present Continuous**
- Incorrect: I am knowing the answer.
- Correct: I know the answer.

**2. Unnecessary use of 'only'**
- Incorrect: He is coming only.
- Correct: He is coming.

**3. Double negatives**
- Incorrect: I don't know nothing about it.
- Correct: I don't know anything about it.

**4. Direct translation from local languages**
- Incorrect: I am understanding.
- Correct: I understand.

**5. Pluralizing uncountable nouns**
- Incorrect: Informations are available.
- Correct: Information is available.

**6. Confusing 'since' and 'for'**
- Incorrect: I've been working here since three years.
- Correct: I've been working here for three years.

**7. Missing articles**
- Incorrect: I have laptop.
- Correct: I have a laptop.

**8. 'Revert back'**
- Incorrect: Please revert back soon.
- Correct: Please revert soon.

#### **Examples**
18. Correct: Please reply at the earliest.  
    Incorrect: Please revert back at the earliest.

19. Correct: I have finished the task.  
    Incorrect: I have finished with the task.

20. Correct: He is very talented.  
    Incorrect: He is very much talented.

## **6. Sentence Structure**

**Simple Sentence**: One independent clause.

- Correct: The system crashed.
- Incorrect: System crashed.

**Compound Sentence**: Two independent clauses joined by conjunction (and, but, or).

- Correct: The server was slow, but we fixed it.
- Incorrect: The server was slow we fixed it.

**Complex Sentence**: One independent clause + one dependent clause.

- Correct: Although the system crashed, we restored it quickly.
- Incorrect: Although the system crashed we restored it quickly.

**Common Mistakes**:
- Run-on sentences.
- Missing conjunctions.

#### **Examples**
21. Correct: I updated the code, and now it works.
    Incorrect: I updated the code now it works.

22. Correct: If you need help, let me know.
    Incorrect: If you need help let me know.

23. Correct: The software failed because of a bug.
    Incorrect: The software failed because a bug.

24. Correct: While I tested the module, the team prepared documentation.
    Incorrect: While I tested the module the team prepared documentation.

## **7. Active vs Passive Voice**

- **Active Voice**: Subject does the action.
    - Correct: The developer fixed the bug.
- **Passive Voice**: Subject receives the action.
    - Correct: The bug was fixed by the developer.

Use active voice for clarity, but passive is common for formal reports.

**Incorrect Usage Examples**:
- Incorrect: The bug was fixed.

(Missing 'by whom', but sometimes acceptable if agent is unknown.)

#### **Examples**
25. Correct (Active): The manager approved the proposal.  
    Correct (Passive): The proposal was approved by the manager.

26. Correct (Active): We are testing the software.  
    Correct (Passive): The software is being tested by us.

## **8. Conditionals**

Used to express real and unreal situations.

- **Zero Conditional**: (if + present, present) â facts.
    - Correct: If you press this button, the system restarts.

- **First Conditional**: (if + present, will) â possible future.
    - Correct: If you update the driver, it will work.

- **Second Conditional**: (if + past, would) â unlikely or hypothetical present/future.
    - Correct: If I had more time, I would learn SQL.

- **Third Conditional**: (if + past perfect, would have) â past unreal situations.
    - Correct: If I had checked the logs, I would have found the error.

#### **Examples**
27. Correct: If you need help, I will assist you.
    Incorrect: If you need help, I assist you.

28. Correct: If the code is optimized, it runs faster.
    Incorrect: If the code is optimized, it run faster.

## **9. Reported Speech**

Indirectly reporting what others said.

- Change tense (often backshift).
- Remove quotation marks.

**Direct:** He said, "I will deploy the update."
**Reported:** He said that he would deploy the update.

**Common Mistakes:**
- Keeping tense unchanged.
- Using 'that' incorrectly.

#### **Examples**
29. Correct: She said that she was reviewing the code.
    Incorrect: She said that she is reviewing the code.

30. Correct: He asked if I had any issues.
    Incorrect: He asked do I have any issues.

31. Correct: The manager told us to finish the work.
    Incorrect: The manager told finish the work.
