## 3. Intonation and Stress Patterns

### A. Basics

- **Intonation:** The rise and fall of your voice; signals questions, statements, surprise, anger, etc.
- **Stress patterns:** Certain syllables in words get more emphasis (e.g., phoTOgraph vs. PHOtograph).

### B. Common Differences in Indian English

- Flattened intonation.
- Syllable timing instead of stress timing.
- Over-pronouncing every syllable.

### C. Practice Exercises

1. Mark stress in sentences and practice:
    - I would LIKE to meet you TOMORROW.
    - This is my NEW project.
2. Record and listen to your intonation.
3. Imitate native speakers (BBC Learning English, TED Talks).

## 4. Reducing Mother Tongue Influence (MTI)

### A. Identifying MTI

- Mixing 'v' and 'w' (very vs. wary)
- Replacing 'th' with 't' or 'd' (thin as 'tin'; this as 'dis')
- Rolling 'r', omitting articles ('the', 'a'), or using them incorrectly

### B. Exercises to Reduce MTI

1. Practice minimal pairs: *West* vs. *Vest*, *Thing* vs. *Ting*.
2. Slow down speech, articulate clearly.
3. Record your speech and note patterns to correct.
4. Learn to use articles and prepositions accurately.

## 5. Tongue Twisters for Practice

Tongue twisters help with clarity, speed, and accuracy.

**Sample Twisters:**
1. She sells sea shells by the sea shore.
2. A proper cup of coffee from a proper copper coffee pot.
3. The thirty-three thieves thought that they thrilled the throne throughout Thursday.
4. Red lorry, yellow lorry, red lorry, yellow lorry.

**Exercise:** Repeat each tongue twister 10 times slowly, then increase speed.

## 6. Thinking in English: How to Stop Translating

### Strategies

- **Label your environment in English** ("I am picking up my laptop.").
- **Narrate your actions aloud or in your mind.** ("Now I'm checking my email.").
- **Diary/Journal Writing:** Write a short daily entry in English.
- **Practice inner monologues:** When you see an object, think of the English name, description, and use it in a sentence.

### Exercises

- Choose 10 common phrases you say in your mother tongue. Find the English equivalents and use them.
- Speak to yourself while doing routine activities.
- Use apps like ELSA Speak or HelloTalk for conversational practice.

## 7. The Shadowing Technique: How to Use and Practice

### Steps:

1. **Select an Audio Clip (1-2 minutes):** Choose one with a transcript (TED talk, news report).
2. **Listen Once Carefully.**
3. **Play Again and Speak Aloud at the Same Time:** Imitate the speed, rhythm, and intonation.
4. **Record Yourself; Compare and Correct.**

## 10. Confident Participation in Meetings

### A. Useful Phrases

- "I'd like to add something here."
- "Could you clarify that point?"
- "From my experience..."
- "As per our previous discussion..."
- "Let me summarize the discussion."

### B. Tips

- Prepare talking points beforehand.
- Practise clear introductions and closings.
- If you don't understand, ask politely: "Sorry, could you please repeat that?"

**Exercise:** Simulate meetings with friends/colleagues; record and self-review.

---

## 11. How to Give Technical Presentations

### A. Structuring Your Talk

1. **Introduction:** Who you are, topic overview.
2. **Background/Problem Statement**
3. **Solution/Implementation**
4. **Results/Impact**
5. **Conclusion/Next Steps**

### B. Presentation Tips

- Keep slides simple; focus on talking points.
- Practise aloud multiple times, ideally in front of an audience (even virtual).
- Anticipate potential questions and prepare answers.

### C. Exercises

1. Prepare a 3-slide presentation; record and review.
2. Present to a friend or mentor; seek feedback.
3. Watch experts (e.g., Google Talks, AWS re:Invent) and mimic their pacing, transitions.
